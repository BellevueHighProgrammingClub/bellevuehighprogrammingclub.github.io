Events & Opportunities

Intro

Coding competitions are one of the best ways to improve your coding skills, and it's a lot of fun to compete in with your friends. There are multiple contests held throughout the year by various organizations, so check the table below to register for an upcoming competition!

(Most contests are done through Java, C++, C#, and Python.)

Contests

Name & Link When Description/Comments
Advent of Code December 1-25, 2020 puzzles are released at 9pm PST every night Every day, leading up to December 25th, there is a new coding challenge released. The level of difficulty ranges for a variety of skill sets so I recommend checking it out, maybe once winter break begins, or to get in the holiday spirit. As a side note, it is Santa/Christmas themed, but not religious.
UASCO Multiple times a year I generally find the questions more confusing in this contest, and during the contest you aren’t allowed to see the test cases. It is separated by different ranks of difficulty.
CEMC Once a year for each difficulty Computer contests sponsored by the university of Waterloo, it has 2 different sets of difficulty, senior and junior, the junior being the easier one, while the senior is more challenging
Hacker Rank Once a month They happen every month, so it’s good practice for anyone who is interested in computer science, or just wants to get frequent practice in a contest environment.
Google's Code Jam Once a year Once a year, Google holds a coding contest, primarily focused on algorithms. It’s suppose to fun and has a big prize pool of $15,000 for the winner.
