Events & Opportunities

Intro

Most contests are done through C, C++, Python and Java. Because of this, you probably should master one of these languages if you want to have a good time competing in these contests.

Contests

Name Language(s) When Link Description/Comments
Usaco Multiple times a year http://www.usaco.org/index.php I generally find the questions more confusing in this contest, and during the contest you aren’t allowed to see the test cases. It is separated by different ranks of difficulty.
CEMC Once a year for each difficulty https://cemc.math.uwaterloo.ca/contests/computing.html Computer contests sponsored by the university of Waterloo, it has 2 different sets of difficulty, senior and junior, the junior being the easier one, while the senior is more challenging
HackerRank Once a month https://www.hackerrank.com/contests They happen every month, so it’s good practice for anyone who is interested in computer science, or just wants to get frequent practice in a contest environment.
Google's Code Jam Once a year https://codingcompetitions.withgoogle.com/codejam Once a year, Google holds a coding contest, primarily focused on algorithms. It’s suppose to fun and has a big prize pool of $15,000 for the winner.
